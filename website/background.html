Experimental protocol

The experimental protocol for measuring the amounts of mutant (m) and total (N) mtDNA is based on quantitative PCR. This is a technique for amplifying a known segment from an initial set of DNA molecules (more details in Background).

For a single cell, we measure the two quantities m and N by splitting the contents of a cell into two wells, then measuring m/2 in the first well and N/2 in the other. For each of the two wells, a qPCR primer is used to identify and amplify either a DNA segment specific to mutant mtDNA, or a segment present in any mtDNA molecule. *Is this true for total?*

The resulting data consists of amplification curves for each of the two wells. We use this data to estimate and model the measurement error in the initial number of molecules m/2 and N/2.
